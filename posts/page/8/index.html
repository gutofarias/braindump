Idea I had for extending Davies Method which works only for Differential Kinematics and Statics. I wish to extend the formulation to include Dynamics. My idea is to go through Hyper-Dual Quaternions. But I should take a look at Screw Calculus as well. Start with a mechanism or robot (can be either a Open Kinematic Chain System or a Closed Kinematic Chain System) Enumerate the joints and links Use letters for the joints and numbers for the links.

Elm
Jan 1, 0001

A Functional Programming language made to write Web Applications, or I think maybe more like front-end stuff. The language emphasis is on easy of use, no been overly complicated. Has a syntax similar to Haskell. I did some reseach in the topic so to produce something support material for my classes and a reason to practice Haskell. Elm although is not as haskellish as Purescript, turns out to be way simpler to work, as far as I could tell.
